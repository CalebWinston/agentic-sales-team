## Email Templates

### Cold Email Framework

```
Subject: [3-5 words, lowercase, curiosity]

[First line about THEM - observation or question]

[One paragraph - the insight or value]

[One clear CTA - make it easy to say yes]

[Sign-off]
```

**Constraints:**
- 50-75 words max
- 3-4 sentences
- No bullet points (looks like template)
- No "I hope this finds you well"
- No "My name is..."

### Subject Line Rules
- 3-5 words max
- Lowercase (looks personal)
- Create curiosity, not clickbait
- Examples: "quick question", "saw your post", "47 days"

### First Line Bank

**Signal-based:**
- "Saw you're hiring [X] - that usually means [insight]."
- "Congrats on the [funding/promotion] - [specific observation]."
- "Your post about [topic] got me thinking..."

**Problem-based:**
- "Most [role]s I talk to struggle with [problem]."
- "[Company] solving [problem] - is [specific challenge] part of that?"

**Social proof:**
- "Helped [similar company] with [result]."
- "[Mutual connection] mentioned you might be dealing with [problem]."

## Follow-up Sequence Structure

### Day 0 - Initial (50-75 words)
Full pitch, best angle, low-friction CTA

### Day 3 - New Angle (30-40 words)
Different value prop or use case
"Following up - also wanted to mention..."

### Day 7 - Value Add (30 words)
Share something useful, no direct ask
Article, insight, or observation

### Day 10 - Brief Check (20 words)
"Still relevant?"
"Worth a quick chat?"

### Day 14 - Breakup (15-20 words)
Graceful exit, leave door open
Often gets best response

## LinkedIn Post Framework

```
[Hook - first line that stops the scroll]

[Story - 2-3 short paragraphs, personal and specific]

[Insight - what you learned]

[CTA - question or clear next step]
```

**Hook examples:**
- "I lost a $100k deal last week."
- "Unpopular opinion:"
- "The best cold email I ever sent was 12 words."

**Format rules:**
- Lots of white space
- Short lines (mobile-first)
- No walls of text
- Personal > corporate
